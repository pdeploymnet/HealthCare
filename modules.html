Module-1

A. What is Ethics; Conflicts Faced
We will look at ethics and their importance to health care in Module 1. Ethically, it means a set of moral principles which guide human behavior and distinguish between right and wrong. Ethical considerations are of key importance for the healthcare industry, given the considerable impact on patients' lives resulting from decisions. We examine various ethical theories, such as utilitarianism, deontology and virtue ethics, which have been used to make healthcare decisions. Conflicts are an inevitable part of ethical dilemmas faced in healthcare. These may include decisions related to patient autonomy, end-of-life care, allocation of scarce resources, and confidentiality breaches. Understanding and navigating these ethical conflicts are essential for healthcare professionals to make well-informed and morally justifiable choices.
